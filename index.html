Mixed Martial Arts (MMA), sometimes referred to as cage fighting,no holds Often I get asked by friends and students if they should be doing Weight Training for MMA on top of sparring, rolling, hitting pads etc. My answer is always yes. The next question is always, “what is a good weight training routine for MMA?”. So here is a simple but effective weight training routine for MMA mixed martial arts. This routine is not only great for Weight Training for MMA but also will supplement your technical training in Muay Thai kickboxing, Krav Maga, BJJ Brazilian Jiu Jitsu, and Kali. Weight training in general is important for health and fitness. When you’re stronger and have a great weight lifting routine included in your physical activity, all areas of your life will benefit (mental health as well as physical health).
There are 3 strength workouts that require at least 1 day of rest in between. For example you could do workout 1 on Monday, rest Tuesday, workout 2 on Wednesday, rest Thursday, and workout 3 on Friday.
Workout 1
Squat – 3 sets of 5
Bench Press – 3 sets of 5
Rows – 3 sets of 5
Workout 2
Deadlift – 3 sets of 3
Military Press – 3 sets of 5
Pullups – 3 sets of 10
Workout 3
Step-ups – 3 sets of 10
Push Press or Incline Press or Dips – 3 sets of 5
Power Cleans or Power Snatches – 5 sets of 3
This is a great routine for Weight Training for MMA but it can be tweaked, changed, or completely redone to fit your fitness and martial arts goals. It is common to split the 3 workouts into a pushing day (chest), a pulling day, (back), and of course leg day. The workout above mixes up the muscle groups so you are not totally destroyed and unable to train the next day.

Mixed Martial Arts (MMA), sometimes referred to as cage fighting,no holds barred (NHB),and ultimate fighting,is a full-contact combat sport based on striking, grappling and ground fighting, incorporating techniques from various combat sports from around the world.The first documented use of the term mixed martial arts was in a review of UFC 1 by television critic Howard Rosenberg in 1993.The question of who actually coined the term is subject to debate
